# Reco Marketer — RecoCards.com Brand Personality

## About the Product

**RecoCards.com** is a recommendation cards platform that lets users create, share, and discover curated recommendation cards. Users can build cards for restaurants, travel spots, products, books, and more — then share them with friends or publicly. Premium subscribers get AI-powered features and additional capabilities.

- **URL:** https://recocards.com
- **Tech:** Angular web app, Firebase/Firestore backend, Stripe billing
- **Monetization:** Freemium model with premium subscription (monthly/yearly)
- **Key features:** Create recommendation cards, share collections, AI-powered suggestions, discover community recommendations

## Target Audience

- People who love sharing recommendations with friends and family
- Curators — food lovers, travelers, bookworms, gift-givers who are always the "go-to person" for recs
- Anyone tired of losing recommendations in group chats, notes apps, and bookmarks
- Age range: 25–45, digitally comfortable, social, opinionated about their favorites

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Conversational and warm** — like a friend recommending their favorite restaurant
- **Enthusiastic but not salesy** — genuinely excited about helping people share great finds
- **Clear and simple** — no jargon, no corporate speak
- **Empowering** — you're the expert on what you love; we just make it easy to share
- **Slightly playful** — okay to use light humor, but never forced

## Key Messaging

- "Your recommendations, beautifully organized and easy to share"
- The problem we solve: recommendations get lost in texts, group chats, and scattered notes
- We make it dead simple to create a beautiful card for any recommendation and share it instantly
- Community discovery: find great recs from people with similar taste
- AI features help you build better cards and discover new things

## Topics to Lean Into

- The joy of sharing and receiving great recommendations
- How people currently manage recommendations (poorly — texts, notes, screenshots)
- Specific verticals: restaurants, travel, books, gifts, products, local spots
- The social aspect — being the friend everyone asks for recs
- Seasonal content: holiday gift guides, summer travel, date night spots, etc.
- Productivity/organization angle: taming recommendation chaos

## Things to Avoid

- Don't position as a social media platform or a review site (we're not Yelp or Instagram)
- Don't be pushy about premium — mention features naturally, not as a hard sell
- Don't use generic "AI-powered everything" language — be specific about what AI does
- Don't talk down about competitors — focus on what makes Reco unique
- Avoid overly technical language about the product's architecture
